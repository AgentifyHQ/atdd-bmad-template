The IPCC's March 2026 report shows global temperatures have risen 1.2 degrees Celsius since pre-industrial times and may exceed 1.5 degrees by 2030 without emission cuts. Key findings include a 15% rise in extreme weather events, accelerating ice sheet loss, and the energy sector contributing 73% of greenhouse gas emissions. The report recommends transitioning to renewable energy, carbon capture, and binding international agreements. Inaction could cost $23 trillion by 2050 versus $4 trillion for mitigation. The 1.5-degree target is still achievable if emissions peak before 2027 and drop 43% by 2030.
